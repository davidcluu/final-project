First Things, First

A Primer on the U.S. House of Representatives

What is a Representative?

A representative, also known as a congressman or congresswoman, is an elected official who serves a two-year term in the United States House of Representatives.

Each member of the House represents an allocated number of constituents as determined by the population of the state they represent. California holds 53 seats in the House, with 3 representatives from San Diego’s congressional districts.

House Leadership

Key leaders in the House includes the speaker, majority and minority leaders, and whips. The speaker acts as leader of the House and is selected from the majority party.

Majority and minority leaders represent their respective parties on the House floor while whips assist their party’s leadership in managing the legislative program on the House floor.

House Committees

The House has 20 standing committees, all which considers bills and issues and recommends measures for consideration by the House.

The Rules Committee, has traditionally been weighted in favor of the majority party, controls what bills go to the House Floor and the terms of debate.

Currently...

California, the most populous state, holds 53 seats in the House, with 3 representatives from San Diego’s congressional districts. The majority party is the Republican party, holding 246 seats with the total number of voting representatives set in the Constitution at 435.

So What?

Representatives have the responsibility of representing the interests of their constituents and who you vote into office helps determine the majority/minority party in the House. By getting involved in local government, you’re helping to determine the issues that are debated in Congress and passed into law.
